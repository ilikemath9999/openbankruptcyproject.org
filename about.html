Our Mission

We exist to promote transparency in federal bankruptcy courts through free tools, empirical research, and evidence-based policy advocacy. Every tool we build is free, open source, and available to anyone -- debtors, attorneys, judges, researchers, journalists, and policymakers.

We do not sell data. We do not harvest emails. We do not accept attorney referral fees. We do not run ads. We are funded entirely by voluntary donations from individuals who believe the public deserves better access to court data.

Origin

The Open Bankruptcy Project was founded by a consumer bankruptcy debtor who discovered systemic problems and decided to fix them. Analysis of 4.9 million federal cases revealed that 391,951 repeat filers received a Chapter 13 discharge with zero eligibility verification under Section 1328(f). The statutory safeguard existed on paper but was not being enforced in practice -- across any of the 94 federal bankruptcy districts.

We built the first free screening tool to check discharge eligibility against the actual federal case database. We shared it publicly, and the response showed a massive unmet need for free, unbiased bankruptcy information. What started as a single screener grew into a 50-domain network covering every major section of the Bankruptcy Code.

Within weeks of launch, our empirical research was accepted by the Advisory Committee on Bankruptcy Rules as Suggestion 26-BK-3 -- a formal proposal to require attorney-level outcome disclosure in consumer bankruptcy cases.

Governance

The Open Bankruptcy Project is led by a small volunteer board of directors. All board members serve without compensation. The organization's bylaws permanently prohibit sale, merger, or acquisition -- ensuring the project remains independent and mission-driven in perpetuity.

Our Values

Free Access

Every tool, every page, every dataset -- free to use, forever. No paywalls, no sign-ups, no premium tiers.

Open Source

Our code is public on GitHub. Anyone can audit it, fork it, or improve it.

No Attorney Referral Fees

We do not accept referral fees from attorneys or law firms. Our recommendations are based on data, not payments.

No Data Harvesting

We do not collect personal information. No email capture, no tracking cookies beyond basic analytics, no user profiles.

No Corporate Sponsors

We are funded by individual donations. No law firm sponsors, no creditor sponsors, no conflicts of interest.

Permanent Independence

Our bylaws permanently prohibit sale, merger, or acquisition of the organization. The project cannot be bought.
